A 12-key chording layout where every character is a two-key pair and the optimal assignment is measured from your own reaction time — technology shaped to the person, not the other way around.
The Problem

Every keyboard layout — Qwerty, Dvorak, Colemak — optimises for a statistical average person. That person doesn't exist. The layout is universal; the hands using it are not. The result is friction that compounds over a lifetime of typing.

The Engine

12 physical keys. Every character typed as a two-key sequence: first key selects a list, second selects from it. 144 unique characters from 144 pairs. A measurement phase records latency per pair; an optimiser assigns frequent characters to fast pairs. The layout is personal, revisable, and exportable.

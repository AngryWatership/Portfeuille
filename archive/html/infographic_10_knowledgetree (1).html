Knowledge Tree
Most people never needed a knowledge map — researchers did, because the work demanded it. KnowledgeTree makes the scholar's map available to everyone: ideas are sporadic and scattered, and without a tracking system, you cover the same ground repeatedly without knowing it.
The Problem

PhD students spend months on research that has already been done. Companies duplicate R&D across divisions. Journalists miss the stories no one is telling. Without a map of what exists, you cannot know what's missing. Explorers were forced to map by physics. Researchers imposed mapping on themselves. Everyone else navigates blind.

The Engine

Documents (PDF, LaTeX, text) are ingested and semantically indexed via NLP embeddings into a knowledge graph. A Gap Detection module surfaces empty branches — areas no research has reached. An Interest Matcher aligns student passions to unexplored territory. Outputs: PhD positioner (avoid redundancy), pathway suggester (for undergrads), institutional knowledge map (for talent attraction).
